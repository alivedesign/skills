# Storytelling Masterclass (Condensed)

## Psychology of Attention

### The 3-Second Rule
- 65% who watch first 3 seconds continue for 10+
- 45% watch 30+ seconds if hooked
- Brain processes visual in 13 milliseconds

### Two-System Brain
- System 1: Fast, emotional, automatic (hooks target this)
- System 2: Slow, deliberate, logical

## Story Structures

### Three-Act Structure
- Act 1 (25%): Setup
- Act 2 (50%): Confrontation
- Act 3 (25%): Resolution

### Hero's Journey (Compressed)
1. Status Quo
2. Disruption
3. Struggle
4. Transformation
5. New Reality

### Pixar Story Spine
```
Once upon a time...
Every day...
One day...
Because of that...
Until finally...
And ever since then...
```

## Character Creation

### 5 Pillars of Empathy
1. Vulnerability
2. Undeserved Misfortune
3. Relatable Flaws
4. Clear Wants & Stakes
5. Agency & Action

### Essential Questions
- Want? (External goal)
- Need? (Internal, often opposite)
- Wound? (Past trauma)
- Lie? (False belief)
- Fear? (Deep, personal)

## Conflict & Tension

**Tension = Desire × Obstacle × Stakes × Uncertainty**

### Four Types
1. Character vs. Character
2. Character vs. Self
3. Character vs. Society
4. Character vs. Nature/Fate

### Techniques
- Ticking Clock
- Dramatic Irony
- The Dilemma
- Escalation
- Near-Miss

## Dialogue Rules

Every line must:
1. Reveal character
2. Advance plot
3. Create conflict
4. Provide information (disguised)

If none, cut it.

## Plot Twists

A twist works when both **unexpected** AND **inevitable**.

### 10 Types
1. Identity Reveal
2. Betrayal
3. Reversal of Fortune
4. Hidden Connection
5. False Reality
6. Red Herring
7. Moral Twist
8. Timeline Twist
9. Survivor Twist
10. Context Reframe
