## 2. The Famous Person/Company Hook

**Template:**  
The [role/title] of [well-known person/company] used to [surprising or unconventional practice].  
Highly unusual but [explain the purpose or effect of the practice].

**Example:**  
> The late CEO of Zappos, Tony Hsieh, used to offer new hires $2,000 on the spot to leave the company.  
> Highly unusual but helped weed out people that weren't a fit.

**Why it works:** Leverages authority and curiosity. People want to learn secrets from successful figures.

**Template:**

[Share where it started - give brief details on any significant events that led up to your surprising experience].

## 6. The Confession Hook

**Template:**  
I [did a deceitful or sneaky thing].  
I [briefly expand on initial statement].  
But that's not the worst bit. The worst bit was this.

**Example:**  
> I lied to a customer.  
> I sold him something he didn't need.  
> But that wasn't the worst part. The worst part was this.

**Why it works:** Vulnerability + escalation. The "worst part" cliffhanger is irresistible.

---

## 7. The Quick Win Hook

**Template:**  
I landed a [high-value opportunity] just [short time] after [starting in my field/role].  
Here's exactly what I did (X steps), opposite to most [role/profession]:

**Example:**  
> I landed a $20K project one month after becoming a copywriter.  
> Here are the exact 10 steps I took, opposite to most copywriters:

**Why it works:** Specific numbers + promise of a contrarian framework. Offers clear, actionable value.

---

## 8. The Contrarian Advice Hook

**Template:**  
In case you [do this thing in your industry/field].  
Don't [do this thing that everyone does].

**Example:**  
> In case you hire salespeople from startups.  
> Don't judge them by their quota attainment....

**Why it works:** Targets a specific audience and challenges conventional wisdom immediately.

---

## 9. The Relatable Pain Point Hook

**Template:**  
Don't want to [do that thing everyone hates]?  
(Many of you don't)

**Example:**  
> Don't want to be the face of your company on social media?  
> (Many of you don't)

**Why it works:** Validates a common frustration. The parenthetical creates a sense of community and understanding.

---

## 10. The Shift/Change Hook

**Template:**  
[Something] has changed (and [your persona is doing better]).

**Example:**  
> LinkedIn has changed (and small creators are winning)

**Why it works:** Implies insider knowledge about a trend. The parenthetical adds an optimistic twist that draws in the target audience.
